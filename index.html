Online options

We have selected 3 main online course providers to focus on. Although there are obvious aspects of physical classes that you may miss, online classes are still a great tool to learn at your own pace and space. As with all these, you will need to be committed and disciplined!

Local Mosques

Masajids such as Green Lane Masjid and Birmingham Central Mosque also are great sources of knowledge. For more information head over to http://www.greenlanemasjid.org/ and http://www.centralmosque.org.uk/

On site

As suffa Institute

The heirs of the scholars is a 4-year program taught onsite open to brothers and sisters. Each year is split into four modules that aim to equip students with an understanding of Arabic as well as various other sciences. In your first year you will cover the following:

  • Quranic Arabic
  • Sarf (Arabic morphology)
  • Seerah
  • Spoken Arabic

The following years, the modules change to incorporate further Arabic, Hadith, Fiqh and Aqueedah.

Classes are two days a week lasting 2 hours each.
Fees: £30/Month for students

Head over to https://www.as-suffa.org/archive-modular-islamic-study-course-2016/ for more information and don’t hesitate to contact them for further information if required.

As suffa also runs tajweed courses split into levels of proficiency, for more information contact sisterstajweed@as-suffa.org / brotherstajweed@as-suffa.org


Muath

The Muath Centre for Education, based in the Bordesley Centre offers Arabic, tajweed and seerah studies; each at different levels.

Arabic courses for non arab speakers

The Arabic course at muath is offered in 3 different stages starting from beginners (for people with no previous experience of Arabic) to intermediate and advanced and for those who are more interested in conversation and speaking in Arabic there is a conversational arabic course.

Tajweed course

The tajweed course at Muaath is also offered in 3 stages: beginners, intermediate and advanced. Which caters for complete beginners in reading Arabic to people who want to advance their recitation of the Quran.

Sirah

The "Sirah of the Prophet" course has been designed to cater for complete beginners wishing learn more about the life and achievements of the Prophet Muhammad (peacebe upon him) . This course is also ideal for new Muslim converts wishing to learn about Islam (enrolment begins in September and february and each course is between 18-25 weeks long and attendance up to twice a week).

Head over to http://www.muathtrust.org/Muathadulteducation.aspx for more information and don't hesitate to contact them for further information if required.


Qaf

Quranic Arabic course

This 50 weeks long course aims to help you translate the Quran into English. It teaches grammatical themes and goes deep into the grammatical side of Arabic. It also has a Tajweed portion in every lesson to allow students to brush up on their recitation of the Quran.

Fee: Free (Excluding textbook expenses)
Classes are run every Sunday for 2 hours

Head over to http://www.qaf.org.uk/information.php?info_id=26&osCsid=oulngvtg1c0c8061j5eiolep90 for more information and don’t hesitate to contact them for further information if required.
